Are we there yet ?

The climate of change, as India becomes a globally viable education hub.

Education hub India

Ishika Ray - 6.05.2016

The UNESCO Institute for Statistics (UIS) stated in a 2014 report that India is gradually emerging as a preferred destination for foreign students, particularly for South Asian students. With more than 500 universities and 17000 colleges — 16 of which are ranked in the Times Higher Education’s Top 500 — India is a lucrative study destination that provides quality education at a fourth of the cost of studying in any university in the west. Moreover, India has the 3rd largest English-speaking population in the world, and most courses are taught in English, attracting students from Asia and Africa who cannot speak other European languages, but desire great quality, subsidised education.

After launching programs such as ‘Make in India’ and ‘Skill in India’, the Narendra Modi-led central government plans to launch ‘Study in India’ – a campaign to attract more foreign students to enrol in Indian universities. This ties in with the goals of the Indian Planning Commission’s 12th Five Year Plan, which aims to create the capacity for the admission of an additional 5 million students to the existing 25.9 million students (figures from the year 2011-12).

Is India ready for such an influx? The answer is yes. Institutions such as AIIMS, University of Delhi and Manipal University, LPU have significant foreign/NRI reservations, and admit students from over 74 countries including Nepal, Bhutan, Iran, Malaysia and Sudan. With the proper execution of the Five-Year Plan, more students from overseas can be expected to enrol in Indian institutions.

With a myriad college options for every student to choose from, and 200 of the Fortune 500 companies regularly participating in campus placements at these institutions, there is little doubt that the UNESCO’s projections will soon become a reality.
